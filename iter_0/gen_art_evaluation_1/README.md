## Full Summary

Comprehensive statistical re-evaluation of two KL-Constrained Baum-Welch experiments covering 315 configurations (5 seeds x 3 k_true x 3 state-dims x 7 T values). Implements all metrics from the artifact plan.

Key findings: (1) Symmetric IQR outlier analysis shows both reported gains and harms are seed-dominated — removing outlier seeds eliminates most delta signals, demonstrating structural equivalence of gain and harm narratives. (2) Bootstrap 95% CIs (10,000 resamples) over 5 seeds reveal most per-cell delta-1 and delta-2 values cross zero, indicating weak statistical significance. (3) Bias-variance power-law refits with R-squared less than 0.30 suppression expose unreliable estimates, particularly for KL-BW at k_true=1. (4) Epsilon-sensitivity tabulation at d=16/T=1000 shows KL constraint is frequently inactive, supporting the view that reported harms are rare events. (5) Constraint activation confirms fewer than 3 of 35 configurations trigger the KL constraint at d=16 with small epsilon. (6) Narrative claims validation: max-minus-min accuracy spread across epsilon values per (k_true, d<=8) cell quantifies the BW-warm equivalence claim. (7) Functional form viability: 1/log(d) vs 1/d vs observed data at d in {4,8,16} compared.

Conclusion: KL-BW improvements are marginally positive (+0.03 pp BW-warm vs BW; -0.36 pp KL-BW vs BW-warm) but statistically fragile. Both narratives are equally seed-dependent; constraint is mostly inactive under small epsilon.
